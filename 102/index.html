How To Change Your Oil

Transmission Type

Automatic Transmission

Step 1.

First start by parking your vehicle on a flat, level surface.

Step 2.

Set your emergency brake and chock your wheels. This will keep the vehicle from rolling away while you're working on it.

Step 3.

Jack up the front of the vehicle at the manufactures designated jack point.

Step 4.

Remove the skid plate (if equipped).

Step 5.

Remove the engine oil drain plug and catch the used oil in a suitable container.

Step 6.

Install a new drain plug gasket on the drain plug while the oil is draining.

Step 7.

Once the oil slows to a drip every 5-10 seconds, reinstall the drain plug and tighten it to the manufacturers specs.

Step 8.

Remove the oil filter and replace it with a new filter, making sure to lubricate the sealing o-ring with oil pior to installation.

Step 9.

Fill your engine with new oil, following your manufacturers stated weight and quantity of oil.

Step 10.

After checking the dipstick to verify you've added the correct amount of oil, start the engine. Let the engine run a few seconds until the oil pressure light goes out and then shut the engine off.

Step 11.

Recheck the dipstick to verify the level is still correct. If it is, check the underside for any leaks around the drian plug and oil filter. Reinstall the skid plate (if equipped) if there are no leaks present.

Step 12.

Remove the wheel chocks and disengage the emergency brake.

Congrats, you're all done!
